Ghee Rice Kerala Style - Neychoru Recipe and Tradition

Periyar Thattukada · Kerala Rice Guide

Ghee rice Kerala style, known locally as neychoru, is one of the most celebratory dishes in the Kerala food tradition. It is served at weddings, Eid feasts, family gatherings, and as a special weekend lunch. Unlike the mild pilaf styles common in north India, Kerala ghee rice has a distinct spice profile and a specific set of accompaniments that make it uniquely its own.

Key Ingredients for Kerala Ghee Rice

  • Long-grain basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • Pure desi ghee, at least 3 tablespoons for 2 cups of rice
  • Whole spices: cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, star anise
  • Thinly sliced shallots or onions, fried until deep golden brown
  • Cashew nuts, fried in ghee until golden
  • Golden raisins, optional but traditional
  • Hot water or light stock to cook the rice in

How to Make Ghee Rice Kerala Style

  • Heat ghee in a heavy pot and fry the whole spices until fragrant
  • Add sliced shallots and fry until golden brown, then remove half for garnish
  • Add soaked and drained rice and stir gently in the ghee for 2 minutes
  • Add hot water in a 1.5 to 1 ratio and a pinch of salt
  • Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed
  • Garnish with fried shallots, cashews, and raisins before serving

What Is Served with Kerala Ghee Rice?

Traditional Kerala neychoru is served with a combination of parippu curry (dal), Kerala chicken or mutton curry, pickle, pappadam, and raita. At weddings and feasts it is also served alongside muttakoose (cabbage thoran) and a pickle made from raw mango or lime. The combination is rich, layered, and deeply satisfying.

Why Kerala Ghee Rice Is Different

The key difference in Kerala ghee rice compared to other regional versions is the combination of star anise with the other whole spices, the use of smaller Kerala shallots instead of large onions, and the serving tradition with non-vegetarian curries. In Muslim households of north Kerala, neychoru is often accompanied by chicken biryani-style accompaniments and a raita made with yogurt and cucumber.
