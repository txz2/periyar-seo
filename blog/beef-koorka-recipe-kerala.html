How Is Beef Koorka Made in Kerala? Traditional Recipe

Periyar Thattukada · Kerala Beef Dishes

Beef koorka is one of those dishes that makes you understand Kerala food at a deeper level. It is not just a beef curry. It is a combination of two very specific ingredients: slow-cooked Kerala beef and koorka, a small tuber also known as Chinese potato or Coleus potato, that grows only in certain parts of Kerala and other tropical regions.

What Is Koorka?

Koorka is a small, knobby tuber with a rough brown skin and white flesh. It has a starchy texture similar to baby potatoes but with a slightly nutty, earthy flavour that is unique to it. It is seasonal, appearing in Kerala markets mainly from November to February. When you find fresh koorka, it is unmistakable. Cleaning it is labour-intensive because of the rough skin, which is part of why koorka dishes are considered special-occasion food in many Kerala households.

How Beef Koorka Is Made

  • Koorka is cleaned by rubbing in rough cloth or with a brush, then halved
  • Beef is cut into medium chunks and marinated with ginger, garlic, pepper, turmeric, and chilli powder
  • Both are pressure-cooked together until tender
  • The cooked mixture is then dry-roasted in a heavy iron pan with curry leaves, sliced shallots, and coconut oil until the beef develops a slightly crisp outer edge
  • The final dish is semi-dry, intensely flavoured, and fragrant with coconut oil and curry leaves

Why Is Beef Koorka So Popular in Kerala?

The combination works because koorka absorbs the beef juices and spice marinade during cooking, becoming deeply flavoured while holding its texture. Unlike potatoes which become soft and floury, koorka stays firm and slightly chewy, giving the dish a satisfying bite alongside the tender beef.

It is also a dish that improves the next day, as the flavours develop further overnight. Many Kerala households consider day-old beef koorka, reheated in coconut oil, better than freshly made.
