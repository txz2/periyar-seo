Kerala Breakfast Guide - From Puttu to Appam

Periyar Thattukada · Kerala Food Guide

Kerala breakfast is one of the most diverse and satisfying morning food cultures in India. Unlike many other regional cuisines where breakfast is a single signature dish, Kerala has a full range of breakfast foods that vary by community, religion, region, and season. This guide covers every classic Kerala breakfast dish so you know exactly what to order when you are in Kerala or visiting a nadan Kerala eatery.

Puttu - The Iconic Kerala Morning Staple

Puttu is a cylindrical steamed rice cake made with rice flour and freshly grated coconut. It is the most iconic Kerala breakfast and is served in every district of the state. The classic pairing is kadala curry (black chickpea curry), though it is also eaten with ripe banana, pappadam, and egg curry. Different regions have different textures - Malabar puttu is finer and softer, while central Kerala puttu is coarser.

Appam - The Lacy Rice Crepe

Appam is a thin, fermented rice crepe with crispy lacy edges and a soft, slightly thick centre. It is made in a special rounded pan called an appa chatty. Appam is paired with vegetable stew (the most classic combination), egg curry, or kadala curry. It is lighter and more delicate than puttu and is a staple Sunday morning dish in many Kerala homes.

Idiyappam - String Hoppers

Idiyappam, also known as string hoppers, are delicate nests of steamed rice noodles made by pressing rice flour through a press with tiny holes. They are lighter than puttu and softer than appam, and are typically served with coconut milk sweetened with a pinch of sugar, egg curry, or vegetable stew. Idiyappam is particularly popular in south Kerala and among Syrian Christian communities.

Dosa and Idli - The Pan-South India Classics

While dosa and idli are associated with Tamil Nadu, they are deeply embedded in Kerala breakfast culture as well. Kerala dosas are often thicker and less crispy than Madras-style dosas, and the chutneys served alongside are coconut-forward. Idli in Kerala is often served with sambar that includes small pearl onions and drum sticks, giving it a distinct flavour.

Ada - The Festival Breakfast

Ada is a thick steamed rice dumpling made with rice flour, filled with a sweet coconut and jaggery mixture, and wrapped in banana leaf before steaming. It is a festive breakfast most associated with Onam and Vishu but eaten year-round as a special morning treat.
