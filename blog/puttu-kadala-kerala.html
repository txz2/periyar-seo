What Is the Authentic Way to Serve Puttu Kadala in Kerala?

Periyar Thattukada · Kerala Breakfast Guide

Puttu kadala is one of the most iconic Kerala breakfasts. But the authentic way to serve it is very different from what most hotels or chain restaurants put in front of you. This is the real version: the one served in homes, roadside thattukadas, and traditional eateries across Kerala for generations.

What Is Puttu?

Puttu is a cylindrical steamed rice cake made from coarsely ground rice flour layered with freshly grated coconut. The key word is fresh. The coconut must be grated the same morning, not the day before, not from a packet. The rice flour must have just enough moisture to hold together when steamed but still crumble the moment you press it.

Traditionally puttu is steamed in a bamboo or metal cylindrical vessel called the puttu kutti, which fits on top of a pressure cooker or a special pot. The steam rises through, cooking the rice and coconut together into a firm but crumbly cylinder.

What Is Kadala Curry?

Kadala refers to black chickpeas, also called kala chana. The curry is made by soaking the chickpeas overnight, then pressure cooking and simmering in a thick coconut-based gravy with freshly ground spices including coriander, pepper, dried red chilli, and grated coconut roasted to a deep brown. The result is a dark, intensely flavoured curry with a thick gravy that clings to the puttu as you break it apart.

The Authentic Way to Serve It

  • Puttu should be served immediately after steaming, still hot and slightly moist inside
  • It is placed directly on a banana leaf or a steel plate, never in a bowl where it gets soggy
  • Kadala curry is served on the side or poured gently over the top
  • A ripe banana on the side is traditional, especially the nendran variety
  • Pappadam and black coffee complete the authentic Kerala breakfast plate

Regional Variations

In Malabar the puttu is finer-ground and slightly softer. In central Kerala it is coarser with more texture. In Kuttanad and coastal areas, puttu is sometimes served with fish curry instead of kadala, especially in the evening. The kadala curry spice level also changes by region, with Malabar versions being spicier and more aromatic with the addition of fennel and star anise.
