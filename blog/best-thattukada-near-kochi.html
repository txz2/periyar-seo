What Is a Thattukada?

A thattukada is a small roadside eatery, typically open from early morning or late evening, that serves Kerala home-style food without any pretense. The word comes from thattu meaning a tray or flat surface, and kada meaning shop. Originally these were push-carts with a flat iron griddle, but today a thattukada can be a permanent roadside structure, a stall, or even a small shop as long as it maintains the spirit: fast, affordable, nadan food cooked to order.

What Makes a Thattukada the Best?

  • Food cooked fresh to order, not kept in steel containers for hours
  • Spices ground in-house or sourced from local markets, not pre-mixed powders
  • Coconut oil used for cooking, not refined oil
  • Dishes that change with the season and availability of ingredients
  • No menu pressure to order expensive items, eat what you want

Best Kerala Street Food to Try Near Kochi

  • Beef koorka when in season
  • Appam with stew or kadala curry in the evening
  • Neychoru (ghee rice) as a filling meal at any time
