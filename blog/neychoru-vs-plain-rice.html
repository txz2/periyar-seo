Neychoru vs Plain Rice in Kerala - What Is the Difference?

Periyar Thattukada · Kerala Rice Dishes

If you have eaten at a Kerala feast or visited a good thattukada, you have probably encountered neychoru. But what exactly is it, and how is it different from plain cooked rice? The difference is significant, and understanding it helps you appreciate why neychoru holds a special place in Kerala food culture.

What Is Neychoru?

Neychoru is ghee rice cooked in the Kerala style. The name comes from ney meaning ghee (clarified butter) and choru meaning rice. It is made by cooking basmati or long-grain white rice in ghee with whole spices including cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves, then finishing it with fried onions, cashews, and raisins. The result is fragrant, golden, and rich.

How Is Neychoru Different from Plain Rice?

  • Plain rice is boiled in water. Neychoru is cooked in ghee and spiced stock.
  • Plain rice has a neutral flavour. Neychoru is aromatic with whole spices.
  • Plain rice is eaten every day. Neychoru is served at weddings, feasts, and special occasions.
  • Plain rice pairs with any curry. Neychoru pairs specifically with parippu (dal), pickle, pappadam, and rich meat curries.

Is Neychoru the Same as Ghee Rice?

Yes, neychoru and ghee rice are the same thing in Kerala. The term neychoru is used in Kerala households and thattukadas while ghee rice is the more widely understood English term. Outside Kerala, similar preparations are also called nei choru or nei sadam in Tamil Nadu, but the spice profile and serving traditions differ.

When Is Neychoru Served?

In Kerala tradition, neychoru is the celebration rice. It is served at weddings, Eid feasts, Onam sadyas as a variation, and at family gatherings. At thattukadas and small restaurants, neychoru served with beef curry or mutton curry is one of the most satisfying meals you can eat.
